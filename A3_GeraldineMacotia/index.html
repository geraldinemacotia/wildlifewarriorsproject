Act
Now.

We can take immediate actions to reduce
the most pressing threats to the wildlife.

Pollution

Air pollution harms animals because it damages their respiratory systems and produces greenhouse gases, which contribute to global warming.

Droughts

Plants and animals, like humans, rely on water. When there is a drought, their food source may be reduced, and their environment may be harmed.

Deforestation

Deforestation can result in the loss of habitat for wildlife as well as a general deterioration of their environment.

Illegal animal trafficking

Poachers, traffickers, and well-organized criminal syndicates are relentlessly pursuing profit at any cost to fulfill consumer demand in the illegal wildlife trade.

Flood

Floodwater can destroyed wildlife habitats and pollute rivers and habitats. They may cause plants to succumb to water inundation.

Fire

Fire may be devastating, destroying houses, wildlife habitat, and wood, as well as contaminating the air with hazardous pollutants.
